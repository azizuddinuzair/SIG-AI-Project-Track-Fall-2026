GA Research & Testing Learnings (Mar 4, 2026)

Context
- Project: Pokemon GA team optimization
- Goal: verify optimization quality, prevent metric drift, and lock practical config choices

What we tested
1) Phase 3 system validation
- Random baseline comparison (GA vs random sampling)
- Local-optimum sharpness using neighbor swaps
- Entropy overfitting check
- Fitness component scaling checks
- Multi-seed stability checks

2) Phase 2 ablation and sensitivity
- Entropy/diversity weight sensitivity sweep
- Component ablation (remove individual terms)
- Initialization method sensitivity
- Additional neighborhood robustness probes

3) Final short validation run
- Config: inverse initialization, entropy enabled, weakness_lambda = 0
- Quick stability with 2 seeds

Critical engineering practices learned
- Single source of truth for config access is essential (nested keys must be used consistently).
- Always re-compute fitness from team + config and assert equality against stored best fitness.
- Added strict consistency tolerance: 1e-6 to catch silent drift early.
- Use deep copy for nested config mutations inside experiments.

Key findings
- GA consistently beats random baseline (material gap).
- Entropy/diversity term is important; removing it causes large fitness drop.
- Weakness penalty (as previously tuned) is net harmful; setting weakness_lambda=0 improved outcomes.
- Inverse archetype initialization performs best among tested initialization strategies.
- Stability across seeds is strong under the selected setup.

Final quick validation outcome (40 pop, 30 generations, 2 seeds)
- Mean best fitness: 0.7540
- Std dev: 0.003808
- CV: 0.51%
- Avg unique types in best teams: 5.5
- Fitness consistency assertion: PASS

Practical takeaway
- Recommended baseline config for next iteration:
  - initialization.method = inverse
  - fitness.diversity_weight = 0.15
  - fitness.weakness_lambda = 0.0
  - mutation.weighted = true
- Next high-value improvement is team-aware mutation pressure (underrepresented archetypes within a team), then re-validate.
